Comprehensive Resources for the IFM Community

The Directorate of Research, Consultancy and Publication (DRCP) is committed to supporting the academic, professional, and personal well-being of all students, faculty, and staff at the Institute of Finance Management. This page serves as a central hub for essential resources and services available across the Institute.

Academic & Research Resources

Access key documents, tools, and platforms to support your learning and research endeavors:

  • Student Information System (EMS) Portal: Your central hub for academic records, course registration, examination results, fee statements, and personal information updates. Access EMS Portal
  • IFM Learning Management System (E Learning) / Moodle: Access course materials, submit assignments, participate in online discussions, and track your learning progress. Login to E-Lerning
  • IFM Library Resources: Explore a vast collection of e-journals, e-books, databases (JSTOR, EBSCOhost, etc.), and the institutional repository for in-depth research. IFM Library Portal
  • Research Proposal Guidelines: Comprehensive guides and forms for preparing and submitting your research proposals and ethical clearance applications. (Link to DRCP internal documents if available or general IFM research guidelines).
  • Thesis & Dissertation Writing Manual: Essential manual for structuring, writing, and formatting your research output according to IFM standards. (Link to DRCP internal documents if available or general IFM guidelines).

Student Support Services

Your well-being is our priority. Access confidential and professional support services:

  • Counselling Unit: Confidential support for academic stress, personal challenges, mental health concerns, and career guidance. Contact Counselling Unit
  • Health Services: Access to the campus clinic for primary healthcare needs, first aid, and basic medical consultations. Contact Health Services
  • Student Affairs Office: For general student welfare, accommodation, extracurricular activities, and general inquiries. (Visit main IFM site for details).

Faculty & Staff Resources

Dedicated resources to support our esteemed faculty and staff in their teaching, research, and administrative roles:

  • Staff Portal: Access HR information, payroll details, internal memos, and administrative tools. Login to Staff Portal
  • Research Grants & Funding Opportunities: Information on internal and external research grants, application procedures, and support for securing funding. (Link to DRCP internal page if available).
  • Publication Support: Assistance with manuscript preparation, journal selection, peer review processes, and dissemination of research findings. (Contact DRCP Publication Officer).
  • Professional Development Programs: Workshops and training sessions to enhance teaching methodologies, research skills, and leadership capabilities.

Alumni Network & Engagement

Stay connected with your alma mater and leverage the power of the IFM and DRCP alumni community:

  • IFM Alumni Portal: Update your contact information, access alumni directories, and stay informed about alumni events. IFM Alumni Portal
  • Alumni Professional Networks: Join our LinkedIn group and other social media platforms to connect with fellow graduates and industry leaders. (e.g., IFM Alumni LinkedIn)
  • Mentorship & Collaboration: Opportunities to mentor current students, engage in joint research projects, or collaborate on consultancy assignments. (Contact DRCP for details).

Cultural & Historical Resources (Museums)

While not directly related to DRCP's core functions, exploring cultural and historical sites can enrich your understanding of Tanzania's socio-economic context:

  • National Museum and House of Culture: Explore Tanzania's history, art, and ethnography. National Museums of Tanzania
  • Village Museum: An open-air museum showcasing traditional Tanzanian tribal dwellings and cultural practices.
  • IFM Archives: The IFM Library also houses historical documents and records related to the Institute's history and the evolution of finance education in Tanzania. (Contact Library for access).
